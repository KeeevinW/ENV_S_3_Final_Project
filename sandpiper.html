Case Study

Sandpiper Golf Course

Located in Goleta, Sandpiper Golf Course serves as the home course for the UCSB Men's Golf Team. The course is situated on the coastal bluffs of the Pacific Ocean, offering stunning views of the ocean and the Santa Ynez Mountains. Being one of the best public golf courses in South California in terms of quality, Sandpiper has done a great job of balancing the needs of golfers and the environment.

Recently, Sandpiper Golf Course has proposed a project designed by Tom Doak, a world-renowned golf course architect and the “green gardener of eco-friendly courses”, as reported by CNN, to contribute to environment improvements. Particularly, over half of the irrigated grass has been replaced with native habitat, providing a more suitable environment for local wildlife and promoting biodiversity. The usage of concrete cart paths and agrochemicals are also significantly reduced to minimize the environmental impact.

Another major focus of this project is to improve water conservation. They plan to remove the existing septic tanks with a sewer system to reduce water usage and improve water quality.

Through this project, a portion of Devereaux Creek is also restored to its natural state. Similar to UC Santa Barbara's North Campus Open Space, this environmental restoration aims to support species that rely on this ecosystem.

Sandpiper Golf Course also participated in the Monarchs in the Rough program I mentioned in Habitat Disruption, a plant palette has been introduced to support Monarch Butterfly foraging.

Overall, Sandpiper Golf Course's improvement project is a great example of how golf courses can be managed in a sustainable way, it shows that it is not impossible to maintain a high-quality golf course while also protecting the environment, as long as the course is managed with the environment in mind.
