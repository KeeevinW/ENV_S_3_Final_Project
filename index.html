Introduction

How Golf Courses Impact the Environment

In China, the golf market peaked in 2014, when the number of golf courses reached more than 1400. More than ten years later, now, nearly one thousand of them have been shut down. Despite the economical and political reasons, the environmental impact also plays a significant role in the decision-making process. They were claimed to be water-consuming, pesticide-using, and causing soil erosion, etc. As a golfer, it was frustrating to witness this decline. I was unconvinced by the environmental accusations, because for me, and a lot of people, golf courses represent grass, trees, and lakes, which are the symbols of nature. Therefore, I decided to investigate the environmental impact of golf courses and build this website to share my findings. This project serves as the final project for ENV S 3 at UCSB, specifically, I will talk about how golf courses influence the environment, both positively and negatively, and propose potential solutions that can mitigate the negative impacts and replace arbitrary shutdown of golf courses. I will also conduct case studies on Ocean Meadows golf course (now UC Santa Barbara North Campus Open Space) in Goleta, CA, and Sandpiper golf course nearby.
