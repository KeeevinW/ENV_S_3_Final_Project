Urban Heat Island Mitigation

According to United States Environmental Protection Agency, heat islands are urban areas where the temperature is significantly higher than its surrounding outlying areas. Since structures such as roads, buildings, and other infrastructure are able to absord and re-emit the sun's heat more than natural landscapes, urban areas, where these structures are highly concentrated and greenery is limited, thus tend to have higher temperature, usually 1-7°F in daytime and 2-5°F in nighttime higher than temperatures in outlying areas.

In urban environment, the extensive vegetation and open space can significantly cool the surrounding air, helping to mitigate the urban heat island effect. Especially as climate change intensifies heat waves and thermal stress in cities, such thermal regulation service provided by green spaces provides tangible environmental and social benefits.
