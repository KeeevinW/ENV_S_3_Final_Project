UC Santa Barbara North Campus Open Space

The UC Santa Barbara North Campus Open Space (NCOS) is a ecological restoration and public access project led by UC Santa Barbara's Cheadle Center for Biodiversity and Ecological Restoration (CCBER). It was launched to restore and protect over 136 acres of wetland and upland habitats on land that previously taken up by the Ocean Meadows Golf Course.

The project began in 2013, aimed to restore the land to its natural wetland and coastal ecosystem, including Devereux Slough, a vital coastal wetland that supports migratory birds, native plants, amphibians, and other wildlife. By rewilding the area, slough has been reconnected to its floodplain, improving habitat quality and climate resilience.

NCOS was also designed to be a community recourses. It features a network of trails, educational signage, and opportunities for research and environmental education. CCBER's Year 3 Monitoring Report has provided an in-depth look at the project's ecological outcome. Generally, the project has met or exceeded almost all of the its performance criteria, including goals for native plant cover and species diversity. For example, their report revealed thatthe threatened western snowy plover has begun nesting on the restored sand flats and burrowing owls are occupying the artificial burrows installed on the upland mesa, which are all promising signs that the restoration is improving the environment.

Now, this open space has became popular among bird watchers. It also serves as a living laboratory for not only UCSB to conduct research but also local community organizations and schools to host educational programs.
